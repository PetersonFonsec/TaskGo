# ADR-001: Deliver the Backoffice Through Complete Operational Verticals

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-07-02

## Context

The Proxi Backoffice must eventually support provider governance, customer and order operations, disputes, payments, service and category moderation, reviews, role-based access, and complete administrative traceability. The first release prioritizes operational security, fixed permissions for Administrator, Support, Finance, and Moderator roles, and direct execution of authorized actions with auditability. Delivering every domain at once would broaden the initial scope and delay operational value.

## Decision

Deliver the Backoffice as a sequence of complete operational verticals. Each domain will include the relevant viewing and action capabilities, fixed role permissions, required action justification, and audit coverage before that domain is released. Domains will be introduced incrementally rather than launching a broad but shallow administrative surface.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Security Foundation First

- **Description**: Deliver cross-cutting access control, provider approval and blocking, and audit capabilities before broader operational domains.
- **Pros**: Establishes a uniform governance foundation early and minimizes uncontrolled administrative actions.
- **Cons**: Delays complete day-to-day workflows and spreads the value of a domain across multiple phases.
- **Why rejected**: The selected approach favors usable end-to-end operational outcomes in each release.

### Alternative 2: Broad Visibility With Gradual Actions

- **Description**: Provide read access across all domains in the first release and add administrative actions later.
- **Pros**: Gives the operations team early visibility into the entire marketplace.
- **Cons**: Increases initial scope, creates incomplete workflows, and may frustrate operators who can see issues but cannot resolve them.
- **Why rejected**: The team prefers complete, governed workflows over broad but passive visibility.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Each released domain provides an immediately usable operational workflow.
- Permission and audit requirements are validated within real administrative actions.
- Scope can be controlled by releasing one business domain at a time.
- Operational feedback from one vertical can improve subsequent verticals.

### Negative

- Some teams will wait longer for their domain to become available.
- Shared governance capabilities may evolve incrementally across early verticals.
- Cross-domain reporting will remain incomplete until later phases.

### Risks

- Early verticals could define inconsistent conventions; mitigate this with shared product rules for permissions, justifications, and audit records.
- Stakeholders may pressure the team to partially add later domains; mitigate this by requiring each release to meet its vertical completion criteria.
- A poorly chosen vertical order could delay the highest-value workflow; mitigate this by explicitly prioritizing domains before drafting the rollout plan.

## Implementation Notes

The technical design must preserve the product rule that every released vertical includes fixed role authorization and complete audit coverage. Detailed architecture and implementation choices belong in the TechSpec.
